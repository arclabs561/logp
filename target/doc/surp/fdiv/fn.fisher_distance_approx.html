Fisher information distance (approximation via Bhattacharyya).

The Fisher information metric defines a Riemannian geometry on the space of probability distributions. The geodesic distance under this metric is related to the Hellinger distance:

d_Fisher ≈ 2 * arccos(BC(P, Q))  (for distributions with same support)

§Connection to Statistical Estimation

Fisher information governs the Cramér-Rao bound for unbiased estimators. For location families, the Fisher information rate determines minimax estimation rates. In two-point testing, distinguishing P from Q requires n = Ω(1/d²_Fisher) samples.

See also: hellinger_squared_tensorized for the tensorization identity that underlies many statistical lower bounds.
